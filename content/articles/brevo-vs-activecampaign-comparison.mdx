## Brevo vs ActiveCampaign: The Full Picture

ActiveCampaign has built one of the strongest reputations in the email marketing and marketing automation space, particularly for its deep CRM integration and highly sophisticated automation capabilities. It positions itself as a "customer experience automation" platform — a tool that goes far beyond email to automate the entire customer lifecycle.

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) is a more comprehensive multi-channel platform that also includes CRM and automation, at a significantly lower price point. For many businesses, especially those with growing lists, Brevo delivers 80-90% of ActiveCampaign's capability at 30-50% of the cost.

This comparison examines both platforms across every major dimension to help you make an informed decision.

## Pricing

### ActiveCampaign Pricing

ActiveCampaign uses a contact-based pricing model and four tiers:

- **Starter:** From $15/month for 1,000 contacts to $419+/month for 100,000 contacts
- **Plus:** From $49/month for 1,000 contacts to $699+/month for 100,000 contacts
- **Professional:** From $79/month for 1,000 contacts to $999+/month for 100,000 contacts
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing

For 25,000 contacts on the Plus plan, expect to pay around $250-350/month. The Professional plan adds significantly more cost.

### Brevo Pricing

Brevo's volume-based pricing with unlimited contacts:

- **Free:** $0/month, 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts
- **Starter:** From $25/month for 20,000 emails
- **Business:** From $65/month for 20,000 emails
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing

A business with 25,000 contacts sending 4 campaigns per month (approximately 100,000 emails) on Brevo Business pays $100-130/month — compared to $250-350/month on ActiveCampaign Plus.

**Verdict:** Brevo is substantially more affordable, especially as lists grow beyond 10,000 contacts.

## Marketing Automation

### ActiveCampaign Automation

ActiveCampaign's automation is widely considered among the best in the industry. It features:

- **Visual automation builder:** Highly intuitive drag-and-drop workflow editor
- **Broad trigger library:** Email opens, link clicks, web visits, contact attribute changes, deal stage changes, form submissions, SMS replies, and over 850 app integrations
- **Conditional branching:** Complex if/else logic with multiple condition types
- **Goals and wait-until conditions:** Move contacts through workflows when specific conditions are met
- **Splits (A/B testing in automation):** Test different branches in automated workflows
- **CRM deal triggers:** Trigger automations based on sales pipeline events
- **Conversations:** Integrate live chat and email into automation

ActiveCampaign's automation depth is genuinely superior for complex, multi-stage customer journeys that span email, CRM, and custom events.

### Brevo Automation

Brevo's automation is solid and covers the majority of use cases most businesses need. It features:

- Visual workflow builder with drag-and-drop interface
- Triggers: email activity, list subscriptions, contact attribute changes, website tracking, API events
- Conditional branching with AND/OR logic
- Email, SMS, webhook, and list management actions
- Wait steps and wait-until conditions
- Integration with Brevo's CRM for deal-based triggers

Brevo's automation is not as deep as ActiveCampaign's — it lacks some of the more advanced trigger types and the goal-based workflow advancement that ActiveCampaign offers. But for welcome sequences, cart recovery, re-engagement, and post-purchase sequences, Brevo handles them effectively.

**Verdict:** ActiveCampaign wins for automation depth and sophistication. Brevo is sufficient for most mid-market automation needs.

## CRM and Sales Pipeline

### ActiveCampaign CRM

ActiveCampaign's built-in CRM is deeply integrated with its email automation. Key capabilities:
- Visual pipeline management (kanban-style deal boards)
- Custom deal fields and stages
- Automated task creation based on deal stage
- Sales sequences: automated email sequences tied to specific deals
- Win probability scoring
- Two-way integration between email activity and CRM records
- Native Calendly integration for meeting scheduling

For sales-driven businesses where marketing and sales need to be tightly coordinated, ActiveCampaign's CRM + email automation integration is powerful.

### Brevo CRM

Brevo includes a built-in CRM (Brevo Sales Platform) with:
- Pipeline management and deal tracking
- Contact and company views
- Task management
- Deal notes and activity history
- Integration with Brevo contacts and email activity

Brevo's CRM is functional but less sophisticated than ActiveCampaign's. It lacks native sales sequences, win probability, and the depth of task automation that ActiveCampaign provides.

**Verdict:** ActiveCampaign wins for CRM capabilities. Brevo's CRM is adequate for basic pipeline management but cannot match ActiveCampaign for complex B2B sales processes.

## Email Design and Templates

Both platforms offer drag-and-drop email builders with comparable template libraries. ActiveCampaign's template collection is slightly larger, and its builder is more refined. Brevo's builder covers all the same core functionality.

Both support HTML editing for advanced customization.

**Verdict:** Roughly equivalent. ActiveCampaign has a slight edge in template quantity and builder polish.

## Contact Management and Segmentation

### ActiveCampaign

ActiveCampaign's contact management is comprehensive:
- Tags and custom fields for flexible contact organization
- Behavioral segmentation based on email engagement, web visits, and custom events
- Lead scoring: assign point values to contact behaviors and attributes
- Predictive sending: machine learning-optimized send times

Lead scoring is a standout feature — it lets you automatically identify high-value contacts based on their accumulated engagement and route them to appropriate sales actions or automation sequences.

### Brevo

Brevo's contact management uses lists, attributes, and dynamic segments. It does not have native lead scoring (though you can approximate it with custom attribute automation).

**Verdict:** ActiveCampaign has a significant advantage with lead scoring. Brevo's segmentation is capable but lacks this specific feature.

## Reporting and Analytics

ActiveCampaign offers more detailed reporting for automation performance, including revenue attribution when connected to e-commerce integrations, conversion tracking, and automation reporting.

Brevo's reporting covers standard campaign and automation metrics adequately. Both platforms provide campaign-level open, click, bounce, and unsubscribe data.

**Verdict:** ActiveCampaign offers richer automation and revenue reporting. Brevo's reporting is sufficient for standard use cases.

## Multi-Channel Capabilities

Brevo has broader multi-channel support:
- SMS (global, all plans)
- WhatsApp (selected markets)
- Live chat
- Transactional email (SMTP + API)
- Landing pages

ActiveCampaign focuses primarily on email and CRM, with SMS as an add-on and a separate live chat product (Conversations).

**Verdict:** Brevo wins for multi-channel breadth and integration.

## Who Should Choose Brevo

- Businesses with large contact lists that need affordable pricing
- Multi-channel businesses that need email + SMS + CRM + live chat in one platform
- Businesses where automation needs are moderate (welcome series, cart recovery, onboarding sequences)
- Budget-conscious marketers who need a capable platform at lower cost
- Non-Shopify e-commerce and B2C businesses

## Who Should Choose ActiveCampaign

- B2B companies with complex, multi-touch sales processes needing deep CRM + automation
- Businesses that need lead scoring as a core marketing function
- Companies with highly sophisticated automation requirements (goal-based triggers, complex conditional logic)
- Businesses willing to pay a premium for best-in-class automation depth
