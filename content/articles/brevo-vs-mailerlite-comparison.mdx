# Brevo vs MailerLite: Which Email Platform Is Right for You in 2026?

Both Brevo and MailerLite have built strong reputations as affordable, feature-rich email marketing platforms. They share a similar audience — small businesses, bloggers, creators, and growing e-commerce brands — but they differ in meaningful ways that could make one a much better fit for your specific needs.

This comparison examines both platforms across pricing, email builder, automation, deliverability, contact management, and support. By the end, you will know which platform is the right choice for your business.

## Quick Overview

**Brevo** (formerly Sendinblue) is a European-founded all-in-one marketing platform that charges based on the number of emails you send rather than your contact count. It includes email, SMS, chat, CRM, and transactional email in one suite.

**MailerLite** is a Lithuanian-founded email platform known for its clean design, intuitive drag-and-drop editor, and straightforward pricing. It has earned a loyal following among bloggers, content creators, and small e-commerce businesses.

Both offer generous free plans and competitive paid tiers, but their approaches to pricing and feature bundling differ significantly.

## Pricing Comparison

One of the biggest differences between Brevo and MailerLite is how they structure their pricing.

**Brevo** charges by email volume, not contact count. This means you can store unlimited contacts and only pay based on how many emails you actually send each month. This model is particularly advantageous if you have a large list but only email a portion of it regularly.

**MailerLite** charges by subscriber count, which is the traditional model used by most email platforms. The more contacts you have, the more you pay — regardless of how often you email them.

### MailerLite Pricing (2026)
- **Free:** Up to 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month, limited features
- **Growing Business:** From $9/month for up to 500 subscribers, unlimited emails, all core features
- **Advanced:** From $18/month, adds Facebook integration, custom HTML editor, dedicated IP
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing for large volumes

### Brevo Pricing (2026)
- **Free:** 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts, basic features
- **Starter:** From $9/month, 5,000 emails/month, no Brevo branding, basic reporting
- **Business:** From $18/month, 20,000 emails/month, full automation, A/B testing, multi-user access
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing, dedicated IP, SSO, custom onboarding

**Winner:** Brevo for businesses with large lists they do not email frequently. MailerLite for businesses with small lists who email often.

## Email Builder

Both platforms offer drag-and-drop editors, but they feel quite different in practice.

### MailerLite's Editor
MailerLite is widely praised for having one of the cleanest, most intuitive drag-and-drop editors in the industry. The block-based interface is modern and responsive. Templates are minimal and well-designed. If you value simplicity and want to produce beautiful emails quickly, MailerLite's editor is hard to beat.

MailerLite also offers a rich text editor, a custom HTML editor, and an AI writing assistant for subject lines and email copy — features that have become increasingly valuable.

### Brevo's Editor
Brevo's drag-and-drop editor is fully functional but not quite as polished as MailerLite's. It supports all standard blocks — text, images, buttons, social icons, dividers — and the template library is solid. Brevo's editor also supports multi-column layouts and responsive design.

Where Brevo pulls ahead is in transactional email templates. If you need to design order confirmations, password reset emails, or shipping notifications alongside your marketing emails, Brevo's unified editor handles both use cases seamlessly.

**Winner:** MailerLite for marketing email design. Brevo for teams that need transactional email templates too.

## Automation

Automation is where the two platforms diverge most clearly.

### Brevo Automation
Brevo's automation builder is powerful. You can create workflows triggered by a wide range of events: email opens, link clicks, website visits (via the Brevo tracking script), form submissions, contact property changes, CRM updates, e-commerce events, and transactional email behaviors.

The workflow canvas uses a drag-and-drop node system where you connect triggers, conditions, actions, and time delays. Multi-branch workflows — where different contacts follow different paths based on their behavior — are fully supported.

Brevo also unifies email and SMS in the same automation workflow, which is genuinely rare at its price point. You can send an email on day one, wait three days, then send an SMS if the email was not opened — all in a single sequence.

### MailerLite Automation
MailerLite's automation builder is clean and easy to use. Triggers include form signups, subscriber added to group, birthday, campaign activity, and e-commerce events. The visual workflow editor is intuitive, and setting up a welcome sequence or abandoned cart flow takes minutes rather than hours.

Where MailerLite falls short is depth. Multi-channel automation (email + SMS) is not available. Behavioral triggers based on website activity require more setup than Brevo. And complex conditional branching is possible but less flexible than Brevo's implementation.

**Winner:** Brevo for complex, multi-channel automations. MailerLite for simple, easy-to-set-up workflows.

## Deliverability

Deliverability — the percentage of your emails that actually land in the inbox — is arguably the most important factor in email marketing performance.

Both Brevo and MailerLite invest heavily in deliverability infrastructure.

**Brevo** has a strong deliverability reputation, particularly for transactional email. It supports SPF, DKIM, and DMARC authentication on all plans. The shared IP reputation is actively managed, and dedicated IP addresses are available on higher-tier plans. Brevo's European infrastructure and GDPR-first architecture may also improve deliverability to European recipients.

**MailerLite** also has solid deliverability. It enforces strict list quality standards — requiring confirmation of opt-in practices before approval — which helps maintain clean shared IP pools. MailerLite sends from multiple IP pools and offers dedicated IPs on Advanced plans.

Independent deliverability studies over the past two years have consistently placed both platforms in the top tier, with Brevo slightly edging out MailerLite in transactional email deliverability and MailerLite performing slightly better in marketing newsletter deliverability.

**Winner:** Tie. Both platforms deliver competitive inbox placement rates for marketing email.

## Contact Management and Segmentation

### Brevo
Brevo allows unlimited contacts on every plan. Contacts are organized into lists and can be tagged with custom attributes. Segmentation is based on contact properties, engagement history, and behavioral data. The built-in CRM lets you track deals, assign contacts to sales reps, and manage pipelines — functionality that most email platforms charge extra for or do not include at all.

For e-commerce, Brevo integrates with Shopify, WooCommerce, and Magento to sync order data and enable behavior-based segments like "purchased product X" or "spent over $100."

### MailerLite
MailerLite uses groups and segments for contact organization. Groups are static categories (like "VIP Customers"), while segments are dynamic — automatically updated based on subscriber behavior and properties. The interface for building segments is clean and easy to use.

MailerLite's contact management is excellent for its price point but does not include CRM functionality. If you need CRM features, you will need to integrate a third-party tool.

**Winner:** Brevo for businesses that need built-in CRM. MailerLite for simplicity and ease of use.

## Integrations

Both platforms integrate with hundreds of tools via native integrations and Zapier.

**Brevo** integrates natively with Shopify, WooCommerce, WordPress, Salesforce, Zendesk, and many others. Its API is well-documented and actively maintained. The Brevo Plugin for WordPress is particularly popular, allowing direct form embeds and list sync.

**MailerLite** integrates with Shopify, WooCommerce, Squarespace, Stripe, Zapier, and a growing library of tools. Its WordPress plugin is also well-regarded. MailerLite's API is clean and easy to work with for developers.

Both platforms are well-integrated into the modern marketing stack. The choice often comes down to which specific tools you already use.

**Winner:** Tie. Both have strong native integrations.

## Support and Documentation

**Brevo** offers email and phone support on paid plans, and live chat on Business and above. Its knowledge base is comprehensive and regularly updated. Support response times are generally good but can vary during peak periods.

**MailerLite** is known for its exceptional customer support, particularly on paid plans. Live chat is available 24/7 on Growing Business and above. The MailerLite Academy — a free video training library — is one of the best educational resources of any email platform. Support quality is consistently praised by users.

**Winner:** MailerLite for support quality and educational resources.

## Who Should Choose Brevo?

- Businesses with large contact lists they do not email frequently (contact-unlimited pricing is a major advantage)
- Teams that need email + SMS in a single platform
- Businesses that also send transactional emails (order confirmations, password resets)
- Companies that want a built-in CRM without paying for a separate tool
- Marketers who need complex, multi-condition automation workflows
- European businesses with strict GDPR requirements

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Bloggers, creators, and content publishers prioritizing a beautiful, easy-to-use editor
- Small businesses and startups with under 1,000 contacts (the free plan is very generous)
- Teams where everyone needs to be able to build and send emails quickly without training
- Businesses that value outstanding customer support and education
- E-commerce brands running straightforward campaigns and simple automations

## Final Verdict

Both Brevo and MailerLite are excellent choices in 2026. The decision comes down to your specific situation.

If your priority is **simplicity, design, and a great editor experience** — choose MailerLite. Its interface is genuinely pleasant to use, and its support team is among the best in the industry.

If your priority is **value at scale, multi-channel marketing, or advanced automation** — choose Brevo. The contact-unlimited pricing model becomes more and more advantageous as your list grows, and no other platform at this price point bundles email, SMS, CRM, and transactional email into one plan.
