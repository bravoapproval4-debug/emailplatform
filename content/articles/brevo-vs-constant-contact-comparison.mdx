## Brevo vs Constant Contact: Side-by-Side Analysis

Constant Contact is one of the oldest email marketing platforms in the industry, with over 30 years of history. It built its reputation on simplicity, strong customer support, and ease of use — particularly for small businesses and non-profit organizations that prioritize accessibility over advanced features.

Brevo is a newer platform (founded in 2012) that has grown into a comprehensive multi-channel marketing suite. It offers more advanced features, more flexible pricing, and a broader channel set than Constant Contact — typically at a lower price point.

This comparison examines both platforms honestly to help you determine which is the right choice for your specific situation.

## Pricing Comparison

### Constant Contact Pricing

Constant Contact uses a contact-based pricing model with two main plans:

- **Lite:** From $12/month for up to 500 contacts to $80+/month for 10,000 contacts
- **Standard:** From $35/month for 500 contacts to $110+/month for 10,000 contacts
- **Premium:** From $80/month for 500 contacts to $225+/month for 10,000 contacts

For a business with 10,000 contacts:
- Lite: ~$80/month
- Standard: ~$110/month
- Premium: ~$225/month

Constant Contact does not offer a free plan — only a 60-day free trial.

### Brevo Pricing

Brevo prices by email volume, with unlimited contacts:

- **Free:** $0/month, unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day
- **Starter:** From $25/month for 20,000 emails
- **Business:** From $65/month for 20,000 emails
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing

A business with 10,000 contacts sending 2 campaigns per month (20,000 emails) on Brevo Starter pays $25/month — compared to $80-225/month on Constant Contact.

**Verdict:** Brevo is dramatically more affordable. For most list sizes, Brevo costs 2-5x less than Constant Contact while offering more features.

## Ease of Use

### Constant Contact

Constant Contact's greatest strength is its simplicity. The platform has been refined over 30 years to be as accessible as possible for non-technical users. Key ease-of-use advantages:

- **Beginner-friendly interface:** Clean, uncluttered dashboard with clear navigation
- **Industry-specific templates:** Pre-designed templates organized by business type (restaurant, retail, non-profit, etc.)
- **Event management:** Built-in event registration and invitation tools — a genuine differentiator for organizations that run events
- **Phone support:** One of the few email platforms that offers phone support on all plans
- **In-person training:** Constant Contact offers periodic local seminars and training sessions

For a small business owner or non-profit administrator who is new to email marketing and wants hand-holding, Constant Contact's support infrastructure is genuinely valuable.

### Brevo

Brevo's interface is clean and well-organized but has more features, which means more navigation complexity for new users. The learning curve is steeper than Constant Contact's, though Brevo's documentation and video tutorials are comprehensive.

Brevo offers email and chat support on most plans. Phone support is available on Enterprise plans.

**Verdict:** Constant Contact wins for ease of use and accessibility, especially for complete beginners. Brevo is still user-friendly but has a broader, more complex feature set.

## Email Design and Templates

### Constant Contact

Constant Contact offers over 200 professionally designed email templates organized by industry. The drag-and-drop editor is highly intuitive and suitable for users with no design experience.

The editor includes strong mobile preview capabilities and built-in image editing tools. Templates can be saved and reused, and the editor supports HTML for advanced customization.

### Brevo

Brevo's template library is smaller (around 40-60 templates) but covers all essential use cases. The drag-and-drop editor has comparable functionality to Constant Contact's — block-based layout, responsive design, mobile preview, and HTML editing.

Brevo's templates are professionally designed and modern-looking, though Constant Contact's larger library offers more starting points for industry-specific designs.

**Verdict:** Constant Contact has more templates and a slightly more intuitive editor. Brevo's editor is fully capable but has a smaller template selection.

## Automation Capabilities

### Constant Contact Automation

Constant Contact's automation has improved significantly in recent years but remains basic compared to most dedicated marketing automation platforms:

- **Welcome email:** Triggered when a contact subscribes
- **Anniversary email:** Triggered on a contact's signup anniversary
- **Automated resend:** Automatically resend to non-openers with a different subject line
- **Basic sequences:** Multi-step email sequences triggered by list subscription
- **Contact activity triggers:** Basic email engagement triggers

Constant Contact lacks advanced conditional branching, multi-step branching logic, behavioral triggers, and website event tracking.

### Brevo Automation

Brevo's automation is substantially more powerful than Constant Contact's:

- Full multi-step workflow builder with visual interface
- Conditional branching (if/else) based on attributes, behavior, and list membership
- Website tracking triggers (requires tracking script)
- Email + SMS in the same workflow
- API-triggered workflows for developer-controlled events
- Contact attribute update actions
- Webhook actions for external system integration

**Verdict:** Brevo wins decisively for automation. Constant Contact's automation is basic; Brevo's is comprehensive.

## Contact Management and Segmentation

### Constant Contact

Constant Contact's contact management uses lists and tags. Segmentation is possible but limited — you can filter by contact activity (opened, clicked), demographics, and tags, but the depth of segmentation is less than most modern platforms.

Constant Contact integrates with major CRMs (Salesforce, HubSpot) for more advanced contact management.

### Brevo

Brevo's contact management uses lists, attributes, and dynamic segments that update automatically. The segmentation depth is significantly greater than Constant Contact's, including behavioral conditions, date-based conditions, and integration with all contact attributes.

**Verdict:** Brevo has stronger native contact management and segmentation.

## Deliverability

Both Constant Contact and Brevo maintain strong deliverability reputations. Constant Contact has a slight reputation advantage from its longer history and strict list quality requirements. Both platforms require domain authentication for best results.

**Verdict:** Roughly equivalent, with Constant Contact having a marginal edge from its established reputation.

## Multi-Channel and Additional Features

### Constant Contact

- Email marketing (core)
- SMS marketing (US only, add-on)
- Social media ad management (Facebook, Instagram, Google)
- Event management and registration
- Donation collection (non-profits)
- Survey and poll tools
- Landing pages

### Brevo

- Email marketing
- SMS marketing (global)
- WhatsApp (selected markets)
- Live chat
- Transactional email (SMTP + API)
- CRM
- Landing pages

For non-profits and event-driven organizations, Constant Contact's event management and donation tools are genuinely useful differentiators. For businesses needing transactional email, CRM, or live chat, Brevo offers far more.

**Verdict:** Depends on use case. Constant Contact wins for event management and social ads. Brevo wins for multi-channel breadth, CRM, and transactional email.

## Who Should Choose Brevo

- Businesses of any size that want affordable, scalable pricing
- Organizations that need automation beyond basic email sequences
- Businesses with international SMS marketing needs
- Companies that need transactional email, CRM, or live chat alongside marketing email
- Growing businesses that expect their list to scale significantly

## Who Should Choose Constant Contact

- Absolute beginners who prioritize phone support and hand-holding
- Non-profits that need donation collection and event management tools
- Small businesses who run frequent in-person events and need registration tools
- Users who need the largest possible template library as a starting point
- Businesses already invested in Constant Contact's social ads integration
